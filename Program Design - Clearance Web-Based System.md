## I. RATIONALE

&emsp;&emsp;&emsp; The student clearance process at Cor Jesu College is currently managed manually through physical forms and logbooks, a process that is time-consuming, prone to errors, and limited in its ability to provide real-time status updates across multiple departments. As the student population continues to grow, it becomes increasingly important to streamline clearance processing efficiently, particularly in coordinating approvals across various offices — including the Guidance Office, Clinic, Library, Prefect of Discipline, Cashier, Registrar, SSC, CJC Store, and Computer Laboratory — as well as Academic Clubs and Non-Academic Clubs. To address these needs, the **Clearance Web-Based System** aims to **digitize and automate the entire student clearance workflow, making it faster, more accurate, and more dependable for students, office staff, and administrators.**

&emsp;&emsp;&emsp; Beyond improving clearance operations, the implementation of the Clearance Web-Based System also aligns with several United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). By enhancing the quality, orderliness, and accessibility of academic support services, the project supports **SDG 4: Quality Education**, ensuring that students benefit from a well-managed and technology-enabled institutional environment that removes administrative barriers to academic progress. Through its innovative digital approach, the system contributes to **SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure** by introducing modern technological solutions that strengthen institutional systems and promote a culture of digital advancement. Furthermore, by enhancing transparency, accountability, and security in tracking clearance transactions across multiple departments, the system supports **SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions**, contributing to a more efficient and trustworthy institutional process.

---

## II. OBJECTIVES

1. To design and develop a web-based Clearance System that improves efficiency, accuracy, and security in processing student clearances across multiple departments and offices.
2. To digitalize the clearance workflow across the following offices: Guidance, Clinic, Library, Prefect of Discipline, Cashier, Registrar, SSC, CJC Store, and Computer Laboratory.
3. To facilitate clearance processing for both Academic Clubs and Non-Academic Clubs.
4. To enable role-based access for students, department signatories, and administrators.
5. To provide real-time tracking of clearance status and pending requirements.
6. To maintain a centralized and secure record of all clearance transactions.

---

## III. TECHNIQUES

1. The proponents (Jan Miko Guevarra and Jan Carlo Surig) will coordinate closely with the various department offices and the Software Engineering Instructor (Mdm. Ciemavil S. Alcain) to ensure alignment with institutional needs and project requirements.
2. The team will gather initial data through interviews, observations, and document reviews to design an appropriate system model.
3. Development activities will utilize collaborative tools such as Google Workspace for documentation, planning, version control, and communication.
4. Regular consultations with the Office Staff and Administrators will be conducted to validate requirements and ensure continuous feedback throughout the development process.
5. The Multimedia Team will assist in creating wireframes, interface mockups, and system demonstration materials.

---

## IV. METHODOLOGY / PROGRAM FLOW

- The Clearance Web-Based System will follow a phased development approach:
    - **Phase 1: Data Gathering & Analysis (February 2026)**
        - Conduct interviews and process observations across clearance-related offices
        - Gather operational data and identify system requirements
    - **Phase 2: System Design & Development (March 2026)**
        - Draft SRS, ERD, DFDs, and UI mockups
        - Begin coding, database creation, and interface development
    - **Phase 3: Testing, Deployment & Evaluation (April – May 2026)**
        - Conduct system testing and debugging
        - Finalize deployment version
        - Present the system to the relevant offices and gather feedback
